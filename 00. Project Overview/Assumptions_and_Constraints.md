# Assumptions and Constraints

## Assumptions

- Users (recruiters) have basic digital literacy and can use dashboards effectively
- Job descriptions are well-defined and structured or semi-structured
- Candidates submit CVs in standard formats (PDF, DOCX, or LinkedIn profiles)
- AI models used for matching are trained on relevant and updated recruitment datasets
- Organizations are willing to integrate AI into their recruitment workflows

---

## Constraints

### Technical Constraints
- AI accuracy depends on quality and completeness of input CV data
- NLP models may misinterpret ambiguous job descriptions or skills
- System requires continuous training and updates to maintain relevance

### Ethical Constraints
- AI must minimize bias based on gender, ethnicity, age, or personal identifiers
- Transparency in scoring must be provided to recruiters
- System should not fully automate rejection decisions without human oversight

### Legal & Compliance Constraints
- Must comply with data privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR-like principles)
- Candidate data must be securely stored and encrypted
- User consent is required for data processing

### Operational Constraints
- Human recruiters retain final decision authority
- System is dependent on integration with existing recruitment platforms
