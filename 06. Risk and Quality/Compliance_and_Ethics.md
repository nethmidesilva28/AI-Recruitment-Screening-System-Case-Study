# Compliance and Ethics
## AI Recruitment Screening System

---

## 1. Purpose

This document outlines the compliance, privacy, fairness, and ethical considerations relevant to the design and use of the AI Recruitment Screening System.

---

## 2. Compliance Considerations

### 2.1 Data Privacy
The system processes candidate personal information such as:
- Name
- Contact details
- Employment history
- Education and skills
- Uploaded CV documents

To protect candidate data, the system should:
- Store data securely
- Restrict access using role-based permissions
- Use encryption for sensitive data
- Retain data only for necessary recruitment purposes
- Ensure user consent is obtained for data processing

### 2.2 Data Governance
The organization should define:
- Who can access candidate data
- How long candidate data is retained
- How candidate data is updated or removed
- How audit logs are maintained for system decisions and recruiter actions

### 2.3 Regulatory Alignment
Although this case study is conceptual, the system should be designed with principles aligned to modern data protection expectations such as:
- Transparency in data usage
- Consent-based processing
- Secure storage and access control
- Responsible handling of personal information

---

## 3. Ethical Considerations

### 3.1 Bias and Fairness
AI-based recruitment systems may unintentionally introduce unfair bias. The system should therefore:
- Avoid directly using sensitive attributes such as age, gender, or ethnicity in scoring
- Focus scoring on job-relevant criteria such as skills, experience, and role fit
- Provide recruiters with human oversight and override controls

### 3.2 Human Oversight
The system is designed as a **decision-support tool**, not a fully autonomous hiring decision-maker. Final shortlisting and hiring decisions should remain with human recruiters and hiring managers.

### 3.3 Transparency
Recruiters should be able to understand the basis of AI-generated recommendations through high-level score breakdowns or matching explanations.

### 3.4 Responsible Use of AI
AI outputs should be treated as guidance to improve efficiency, not as unquestionable decisions. Recruiters should validate AI-generated rankings before acting on them.

---

## 4. Ethical Design Principles

The AI Recruitment Screening System should follow these principles:
- Fairness by design
- Human-in-the-loop decision-making
- Transparency in scoring
- Secure and responsible data handling
- Accountability for recruitment outcomes

---

## 5. Conclusion

Ethics and compliance are critical to the success of an AI recruitment product. The system must balance automation benefits with fairness, privacy, transparency, and human judgment to ensure responsible use in hiring workflows.
